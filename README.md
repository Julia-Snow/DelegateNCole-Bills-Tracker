## HB 419 - Approval of land use applications; residential development

**House Patron:** Cole, N.T.  
**Senate Patron:** [To be determined]  
**Committee:** [Committee assignment]  
**Status:** Continued  

**Description:**  
This bill addresses the timely processing of land use applications related to residential development by establishing a deadline for localities to make decisions on such applications.

**Summary:**  
Requires every locality to take final action to approve, approve with conditions, or deny any land use application for the rezoning or development of property that includes residential development within 12 months from the date of submission.

## HB 420 - Public utilities; reopening rate case in certain instances

**House Patron:** Cole, N.T.  
**House Co-Patrons:** Shin  
**Senate Patron:** [To be determined]  
**Committee:** [Committee assignment]  
**Status:** Continued  

**Description:**  
This bill provides authority for the State Corporation Commission to reopen a rate case under specific circumstances involving public utilities and changes in control.

**Summary:**  
Provides that if an application is filed with the State Corporation Commission for approval of the acquisition or disposition of control involving a public utility and such application is filed within 24 months after the entry of a final order in a rate increase proceeding, the Commission may reopen such rate increase proceeding.

## HB 421 - Eminent domain; condemnation of public waterworks systems

**House Patron:** Cole, N.T.  
**Senate Patron:** [To be determined]  
**Committee:** [Committee assignment]  
**Status:** Continued  

**Description:**  
This bill modifies the process for determining just compensation in eminent domain proceedings involving the taking of waterworks systems by considering rate differences.

**Summary:**  
Requires the body determining just compensation in a condemnation proceeding initiated by a locality for the taking of a waterworks system to consider the difference between rates, fees, and charges for water service.

**[View on LIS](https://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?261+sum+HB421)**

## HB 422 - Water utilities; prohibition of multiple rate increases within 3-year period

**House Patron:** Cole, N.T.  
**House Co-Patrons:** Anthony, Bennett-Parker, Carroll, Clark, Cole J.G., Feggans, Garrett, Nivar, Price, Shin  
**Senate Patron:** [To be determined]  
**Committee:** In Committee  
**Status:** In Committee  

**Description:**  
This bill protects consumers by limiting the frequency of water utility rate increases to no more than once every three years.

**Summary:**  
Prohibits a public utility authorized to furnish water or water and sewer service from filing an application for a rate increase more frequently than once in any three-year period, with exceptions for decreases, adjustments, emergency increases, and extraordinary circumstances.

**[View on LIS](https://lis.virginia.gov/cgi-bin/legp604.exe?261+sum+HB422)**

## HB 1436 - Virginia Erosion and Stormwater Management Program authority

**House Patron:** Cole, N.T.  
**Senate Patron:** [To be determined]  
**Committee:** [Committee assignment]  
**Status:** Continued  

**Description:**  
This bill modifies requirements for Virginia Erosion and Stormwater Management Program (VESMP) authorities by removing bonding requirements.

**Summary:**  
Removes the requirement for a Virginia Erosion and Stormwater Management Program (VESMP) authority to have a performance bond with surety, cash escrow, or letter of credit to enter property for maintenance actions.
